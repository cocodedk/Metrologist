How it works

Three steps from photo to measurement

STEP 01

Photograph the surface

Place your red-white-red-white reference stick flat against the wall, door, or window. Take a single photo, any angle, any lighting.

STEP 02

Mark corners + stick

Tap the four corners of the surface and both ends of the reference stick. Pinch-zoom and the built-in magnifier let you land precisely on each point.

STEP 03

Read the results

Width, height, area, diagonal, and corner angles, with a confidence score. No server call. No account. Works entirely offline.

Accuracy

How close does it get?

±1–5%
Typical error range
92% conf.

Single-view planar metrology works from one photograph. No laser, no tape. Error typically falls between 1 and 5%, depending on photo quality, perspective angle, and how carefully you place the reference stick. For most DIY and trade tasks (cutting materials, ordering tiles, quoting jobs) that's more than enough. The on-screen confidence score tells you when a reading is solid and when it's worth retaking the photo.
